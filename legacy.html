Designing the first decimal banknotes

In 1963 Andrews was invited to submit designs for Australia's new decimal currency banknotes, along with fellow designers Richard Beck, Max Forbes and George Hamori. The selection committee, headed by Russell Drysdale, voted unanimously for Andrews' designs, and on 14 February, 1966, Australia's new decimal $1, $2, $10 and $20 banknotes entered circulation. His designs were bold and each banknote captured a unique aspect of Australian life.

Andrews was subsequently engaged to design the $5 banknote, issued in 1967, and later the $50 banknote, issued in 1973. When polymer notes began to replace paper ones, Andrews served on the advisory panel that oversaw their design. Shortly after Andrews' death in 2001 his friend Alan James noted, 'I know of no other currency that captures the rugged vibrancy and spirit of a place the way ours does. To his continuing credit, the new polymer notes reflect the spirit of the work he did'.
